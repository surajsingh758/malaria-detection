Malaria remains one of the world's deadliest diseases, affecting millions of people annually, particularly in tropical and subtropical regions. Early and accurate detection of malaria parasites in blood samples is crucial for effective treatment and disease management. Traditional microscopic examination of blood smears is time-consuming, labor-intensive, and requires expert pathologists, which are often unavailable in remote areas. This project leverages deep learning, specifically Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), to automate malaria detection from cell images, providing a fast, accurate, and scalable solution that can assist medical professionals in diagnosing malaria infections from microscopic blood smear images.
